GBP Audit Checklist

An interactive optimization tool to audit, resolve issues, and rank your Google Business Profile (GBP) in the top 3 Local Map Pack results.

Profile Audit Status 0 of 20 tasks completed

1. Core Profile Details

  • Google Business Profile guidelines strictly prohibit keyword stuffing in your listing title. Ensure your title contains only your legal, registered business name to prevent suspension.
  • Your primary category represents 60% of search ranking strength. Select the category that matches your core transactional search query (e.g., "Internet Marketing Service").
  • Select 3 to 5 additional categories to capture long-tail query volumes. Do not choose unrelated categories, as this dilutes search engine contextual understanding.
  • Write a 750-character business description. Position your high-volume target keywords in the first 250 characters. Avoid promotional pricing links in description copy.

2. Proximity & Contact Data

  • Open Google Maps satellite view. Verify that the red pin sits precisely on the entrance door of your building. Pin proximity coordinates directly govern mobile search results.
  • Your listing phone number must match the number published on your website's header and footer. Use a local number as the primary contact, and secondary numbers if tracking.
  • Inaccurate listing hours lead to negative review triggers. Synchronize daily operational hours, and proactively update 'Special Hours' during national holidays and seasonal closures.
  • Link directly to your optimized local landing page. Inject custom UTM parameters (e.g., ?utm_source=google&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=gbp) to track dashboard calls inside GA4.

3. Visual Assets

  • Upload a high-quality square logo (minimum 720x720 pixels, PNG format) that represents your business logo icon cleanly to trigger identity snippets in maps.
  • Select a high-resolution 16:9 landscape image of your storefront or primary team. The cover photo should establish instant visual confirmation for customers looking for your office.
  • Upload at least 3 exterior photos showing your building entrance and parking options. Take photos during different times of the day to help customers identify your listing easily.
  • Upload 3+ interior photos highlighting your reception area, workspace, or team in action. Authenticity drives local click-through conversions and builds brand credibility.

4. Engagement & Interactions

  • Respond to 100% of incoming customer reviews within 48 hours. Naturally weave local neighborhood keywords and services into your review responses to feed the indexing algorithms.
  • Publish at least 1 update post every 7 days. Google Business Posts behave like local micro-blogs. Share business updates, new services, or local cases to show active listing signaling.
  • Use the Google Business Q&A feature to post your own questions (e.g. "What service areas do you cover?") and answer them from the owner account to preempt customer doubts.
  • Enable the direct messages panel inside your GBP manager. Set up automated welcome greeting templates to respond instantly, and ensure notifications are synced to mobile devices.

5. Citation & Code Ecosystem

  • Inject structured LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema on your website. The schema must map coordinates, address, name, phone, and hours exactly matching the GBP profile listings.
  • Audit directories on platforms like Whitespark or Yelp. Ensure your NAP is identical on top directories including Apple Maps, Bing Places, Facebook, and Foursquare.
  • Embed live customer reviews onto your landing pages using rich schemas. Google values listings linked to websites showing real consumer feedback via structured markup.
  • Acquire high-quality backlinks from local chambers of commerce, neighborhood blogs, and localized sponsors to cement the proximity and authority of your search footprint.
